# PRODUCT.md

## Product purpose
Personal site of Naman Vashistha, a backend / distributed-systems engineer. It hosts long-form writing (technical deep-dives such as building LimeDB, a chess engine, Proxmox/LXC automation; plus reflective essays on growth and craft), a projects index, and a resume. The site itself is the portfolio: a visitor's impression of taste and rigor is the deliverable.

## Register
brand (design IS the product)

## Users
- Engineers and peers arriving from a search result or social link for one specific post.
- Recruiters and hiring managers scanning credibility quickly.
- Returning readers who follow the writing.
Primary job: read comfortably, then trust the author.

## Brand voice (three physical words)
Engineered, considered, unhurried.

Precise and builder-minded without flexing jargon. Confident, a little opinionated, calm. Depth over hype. Reflective as easily as technical.

## Tone
Plain, exact, low-noise. Lets the work and the writing carry weight. Never breathless, never salesy.

## Anti-references (explicitly rejected)
- Terminal / hacker-green / "matrix" aesthetic. The site used to lean terminal; that identity is being left behind on purpose.
- Generic dark SaaS template (gradient hero, identical icon cards, big-number metrics).
- Neon-glow maximalism.
- Editorial-serif magazine cliché (display-serif + italic + ruled columns).

## Strategic principles
1. Readability first. This is a reading surface; contrast and rhythm beat decoration.
2. Distinctive, not loud. Character comes from typography, depth, and a few signature moments, not from color drench.
3. Dark theme is justified: focused, low-light reading and writing; calm but characterful.
4. One accent (violet) used decisively in signature moments. Tinted neutrals everywhere else.
